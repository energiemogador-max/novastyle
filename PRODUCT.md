# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Moroccan homeowners and renovators aged 28–55, primarily in Casablanca, Rabat, Marrakech, Tanger, Agadir. Shopping from mobile (70%+) while renovating a salle de bain or salon. Decision context: comparing options on WhatsApp, Instagram, and Google. They want to confirm quality before committing — they're spending 1,000–15,000 MAD and won't return a large mirror easily. Secondary: interior designers and architects sourcing for clients.

## Product Purpose

Nova Style is a Casablanca-based manufacturer of premium mirrors and home décor (bathroom mirrors, salon mirrors, shower enclosures, tables, consoles). It sells direct-to-consumer online with WhatsApp as the primary conversion channel. Success means a visitor finding the right product, trusting the quality, and initiating a WhatsApp conversation or placing an order.

## Brand Personality

Craftsman confidence. Artisan warmth. Moroccan premium without ostentation.

Three words: **precise, warm, trustworthy**.

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel like they've found a specialist — not a marketplace, not a generic e-commerce store, but a fabricant who knows their product inside out and will stand behind it.

## Anti-references

- Generic Shopify mirror stores (identical card grids, stock photos, no personality)
- Over-decorated Moroccan e-commerce (gold everywhere, heavy ornamentation, cluttered)
- IKEA flatness (too functional, zero warmth, no craft signal)
- Aliexpress-style pages (trust-destroying, chaotic)

## Design Principles

1. **The fabricant's confidence.** Every page communicates that someone made this with their hands. Show process, show material, show the workshop. Not a marketplace — a maker.
2. **Mobile-first conversion.** WhatsApp is the primary CTA. It must be instantly reachable on every scroll position on mobile. Never buried.
3. **Clarity earns trust.** Price, dimensions, delivery time, guarantee — stated plainly on every product page. No hunting for information.
4. **Moroccan context without cliché.** The brand serves Morocco — city names, local delivery, Arabic as a natural layer — without resorting to zellige patterns or gold filigree as decoration.
5. **Photography does the selling.** The design frames the images, not the other way around. Any design choice that competes with the product photography is wrong.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Arabic text must render cleanly (RTL-aware where needed). High contrast for outdoor mobile viewing in Moroccan sunlight. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
